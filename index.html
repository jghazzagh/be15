Course Objectives

The departmental learning outcome: using fundamental reading skills, apply basic rules of grammar, spelling, capitalization, number usage, and punctuation — and utilize terminology applicable to technical and business writing and speaking.

01

03

Develop proofreading and editing skills

04

Write sentences and paragraphs effectively

05

Identify types of sentences and their structure

06

Define and apply gender neutrality to writing content

The Fine Print (Actually Read This)

Course Policies

To be counted as attending before the census date, you must complete and submit at least one assignment — discussions, syllabus quizzes, first homework, etc. all qualify.

This does NOT count as attendance: emailing the instructor or simply logging into Brightspace and clicking around.

After the census date, missing two consecutive weeks of work without an excused absence notice (before the 60% point of the term) will result in being withdrawn from the course.

Past the 60% point, the instructor will not withdraw you — you'll receive the grade you earned.

Excused Absences Include:

  • Official College functions
  • Personal illness
  • Illness or death in the immediate family
  • Pregnant or parenting protections under Title IX / Texas Ed. Code §51.982
  • Any other absence as outlined in MCC policy

Plagiarism is:

  • Using someone else's work and claiming it as your own
  • Copying without quotes or giving source credit
  • Paraphrasing without credit
  • Expressing others' ideas as your own
  • Fraud in any form

Cheating is:

  • Sharing assignment answers (giving or receiving)
  • Using unauthorized material during a test
  • Group work on individual assignments
  • Plagiarism, including copy/pasting AI output
  • Not following Timed Writing rules
  • Having someone else complete your work
Consequences: First offense = zero on the assignment. Second offense = referred to the Student Discipline Committee.

Generative AI Guidelines

AI Policy

Yellow Light: Some Use Okay

AI can be a useful learning tool — but this course is about developing your writing skills, not outsourcing them. Using AI to think, not to write, is the line.

Acceptable Uses

  • Outlining and brainstorming
  • Generating examples for study
  • Clarifying course materials
  • Grammar and spelling checks
  • Seeking help on specific topics

Not Acceptable

  • Writing emails, papers, or essays
  • Composing discussion board posts
  • Producing any written work for submission
  • Answering questions on tests or quizzes
If you use AI for acceptable purposes, you must:
  1. Provide the exact prompt you used
  2. Cite the specific tool (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
Directly copying AI output into assignments without attribution = plagiarism. Same consequences apply.
